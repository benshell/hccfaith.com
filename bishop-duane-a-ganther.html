Bishop Duane A. Ganther

Bishop Duane A. Ganther is the founding Pastor of Harvest Christian Center in Los Angeles, California. As Pastor, he has served the church faithfully for over nineteen years.

Bishop Ganther began his ministry preparation in 1980 at First Apostolic Church of Inglewood under the leadership of the late Bishop Wayne S. Davis. It was there, while serving as the head of the Department of Evangelism, that Bishop Ganther learned how to walk by faith. In 1993, he came under the tutelage of the eminent Archbishop Wilbert S. McKinley.

Bishop Ganther is a graduate of the University of Southern California where he received his B.S. degree in Religion with a focus on Judaic Studies. He also graduated from Hebrew Union College with a Masters of Arts Degree in Judaic Studies. In addition, he's done course work at Fuller Theological Seminary. These years of spiritual and academic preparation have given Bishop Ganther a highly anointed, practical, and educated approach to the Word of God. In 1994, he founded the Young Eagles, Inc., an organization dedicated to building inner city boys into successful men.

Bishop Ganther is also a Licensed Real Estate Broker and an astute businessman. His certification training includes leadership, Holy Land studies, church growth, real estate investing, and various ministry training. In 1995, he was honored by Mayor Richard Riordan for his outstanding community service.

In addition to various mentors, Bishop Ganther attributes much of his success to his illustrious wife Audry. They have been married for twenty-six years and have two wonderful daughters, Brene’ and Shivon.

Bishop Duane A. Ganther is a visionary leader that lives with high expectations and believes that “all things are possible to him that believes.”
